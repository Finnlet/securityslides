The tale of Fitzgerald's shoe store

Our first tale is that of a where you can order products. This site takes orders and redirects you to a 3rd party for payment. This keeps the site PCI compliant.

Background

The site was first brought online on November 15th, 2012

The site has around 300 orders per month

Fitzgerald's shoe store hired a devshop that did not provide support after the site went live

Fitzgerald's shoe store did not have a full time web person on staff

On a cold dark Monday

Myrtle Wilson, book keeper noticed that they were not getting money in their account.

Their order volume did not change

She called the credit card company who said that they had not gotten any orders in the last 2 weeks.

She went online and placed an order,it seemed to work as it should.

She was very confused

The date was March 11th, 2013

On a colder darker Tuesday, they got help

Fitzgerald's shoe store in a expert, Jordan Baker, who analyzed the site. He found that their payment gateway URL, was not pointing to their payment gateway

How it should work

Customer goes to checkout -> is redirected to payment gateway on third party site -> pays -> gets redirected back to merchant site.

What happened

Somehow the URL for the payment gateway was changed in the Ubercart configuration

Jordan Baker started to look at server logs and found that there was a POST request on the page to change this URL by someone at the company's IP address

Myrtle was fired as the IP was her computer's address.

What really happened

Jordan Baker started to dig more and found...

Someone had used a security vulnerability in Ubercart and added Javascript in a field

Normally the javascript would be escaped, but since the site was not patched for SA-CONTRIB-2013-020 - Ubercart - Cross site scripting (XSS), the javascript executed

Lessons Learned

Always update your site when the security team releases an update. (https://www.drupal.org/security)

If you are dealing with sensitive data (credit card numbers) make sure you have the staff to do so, otherwise think about outsourcing everything.

If collecting monies, check more often then every 2 weeks

XSS can do a lot of damage.
